The nephroid is a plane curve whose name means kidney-shaped (compare nephrology.) Although the term nephroid was used to describe other curves, it was applied to the curve in this article by Proctor in 1878. This and the information below may be verified in Lockwood, pp. 62â€“71 (see References).

Equations

Parametric equations

Parametric equations for the nephroid shown above, with cusps on the x-axis, are given by


\begin{align} \ x = a\big( 3\cos(t) - \cos(3t) \big)\\ \ y = a\big( 3\sin(t) - \sin(3t) \big)\\ \end{align}


When the cusps lie on the y-axis, parametric equations are given by

\begin{align} \ x = a\big( 3\cos(t) + \cos(3t) \big)\\ \ y = a\big( 3\sin(t) + \sin(3t) \big)\\ \end{align}



Cartesian equation

A Cartesian equation for the nephroid is


\begin{align} \ (x^2 + y ^2 -4a^2)^3 = 108a^4y^2 \\ \end{align}


Maple Sample Code 


restart;with(geometry):with(plots):

a:=1;

border:=proc(s);
         square(sq,[point(sq1,-s/2,s/2),point(sq2,s/2,s/2),
                    point(sq3,s/2,-s/2),point(sq4,-s/2,-s/2)]);
         sq(colour=white,printtext=false);
       end proc:

point(top,0,2*a):point(bottom,0,-2*a):
circle(basecircle,[top,bottom],centername=Center):
segment(vertical,top,bottom):

n:=52: #number of pictures to draw
pics:=NULL:pts:=[0,2*a]:
for i from 0 to n do
  theta:=i*2*Pi/n;
  point(centre,3*a*sin(theta),3*a*cos(theta)):
  circle(c||i,[centre,a]):
  point(bb||i,3*a*sin(theta),3*a*cos(theta)-a):
  segment(ss||i,centre,bb||i):
  phi:=theta*2:
  rotation(s||i,ss||i,phi+theta,clockwise,centre):
  b||i:=DefinedAs(s||i)[2]:
  pts:=pts,coordinates(b||i):
  pics:=pics,display(pointplot([pts],style=line), draw([border(5*a),basecircle(colour=blue),b||i,s||i,c||i])):
end do:
display([pics],insequence=true,axes=none,scaling=constrained,view=[-4*a..4*a,-4*a..4*a]);
