In mathematics, a lituus is a spiral in which the angle is inversely proportional to the square of the radius (as expressed in polar coordinates).


Branch for positive $r$

Equations

\begin{align} \ r^2 \theta = k \\ \end{align}

This spiral, which has two branches depending on the sign of $r$ , is asymptotic to the $x$ axis. Its points of inflexion are at

\begin{align} \ \bigg( \theta , r \bigg) = \bigg( \frac{1}{2}, \sqrt{2k} \bigg) and \bigg( \frac{1}{2}, - \sqrt{2k} \bigg) \\ \end{align}


Maple Sample Code 


with(plots):
Pp := polarplot(1/sqrt(t), t = 1 .. 16);
Np := polarplot(-1/sqrt(t), t = 1 .. 16);
display({Np, Pp});
