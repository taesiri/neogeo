Fermat's spiral (also known as a parabolic spiral) is an Archimedean spiral with $m=2$.


Fermat's spiral


Equation

The general equation of Fermat's spiral is given below.

\begin{align} \ r = \pm \theta ^\frac{1}{2}\\ \end{align}

In polar coordinates; the more general Fermat's spiral follows $r^2 = a^2 \theta$


The curvature and arc length

The curvature and arc length of the positive branch of Fermat's spiral are

\begin{align} \ \kappa = \frac{2 \sqrt{\theta} ( 3 + 4\theta ^2) }{ a ( 1 + 4\theta ^2)^\frac{3}{2}} \\ \end{align}

\begin{align} \ \tau = a \sqrt{\theta} 2 \sideset{_2}{_1}F \bigg( -\frac{1}{2} , \frac{1}{4} , \frac{4}{5} , -4\theta ^2 \bigg) \\ \ = \frac{1}{8} (1-i)a B \bigg( -4\theta ^2 , \frac{1}{4} , \frac{3}{2} \bigg) \\ \end{align}

where $\sideset{_2}{_1}F \big( a,b;c;d \big)$ is a hypergeometric function and $B\big(z;a,b\big)$ is an incomplete beta function.

with(plots);
