In geometry, the term pseudosphere is used to describe various surfaces with constant negative gaussian curvature. Depending on context, it can refer to either a theoretical surface of constant negative curvature, to a tractricoid, or to a hyperboloid.


Pseudosphere


Equations

The Cartesian parametric equations are :


\begin{align} \ x = sech(u) \cos(v) \\ \end{align} \begin{align} \ y = sech(u)sin(v) \\ \end{align} \begin{align} \ z = u - \tanh(u) \\ \end{align}


where

\begin{align} \ u \in [-2\pi,2\pi], \ v \in [-2\pi,2\pi]\\ \end{align}



Maple Sample Code 


with(plots);
x := u-tanh(u);
y := sech(u)*cos(v);
z := sech(u)*sin(v);
plot3d([x, y, z], u = -2*Pi .. 2*Pi, v = -Pi .. Pi, grid = [15, 200], title = 'Pseudosphere')

Sage Sample Code 


u, v = var('u,v')
fx = u - tanh(u)

fz = sech(u)*sin(v)
